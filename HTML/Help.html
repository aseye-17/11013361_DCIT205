FAQs

  • The Computer Science program covers a variety of programming languages, including but not limited to Python, Java, C++, and JavaScript. The curriculum is designed to provide a well-rounded skill set to prepare students for diverse roles in the field.

  • Yes, the department actively collaborates with industry partners to provide internship and co-op opportunities for students. These experiences offer valuable real-world exposure, allowing students to apply their skills in professional settings.

  • The curriculum is regularly reviewed and updated to align with the dynamic nature of the tech industry. Faculty members stay informed about the latest trends, and adjustments are made to ensure that students graduate with skills relevant to current industry needs

  • Absolutely! The department encourages undergraduate research, providing students with the chance to work on cutting-edge projects with faculty. This hands-on experience enhances their problem-solving skills and prepares them for advanced studies or industry roles.

  • The department offers tutoring services, coding labs, and study groups to support students who need additional assistance. Faculty members also hold office hours, creating an environment where students can seek help and clarification on course material.
